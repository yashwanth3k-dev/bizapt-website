# MASTER CONTENT GUIDELINES — Bizdaptive POC Edition

## Purpose

Bizdaptive is **not** a reporting tool, a dashboard, or a chatbot that “knows your company.”

Bizdaptive is a **governance-native Decision Intelligence platform** that helps organizations:

- **Understand** what the business actually is (people, products, metrics, goals, how they connect)
- **Explain** why something happened or who decided what — with evidence, not guesswork
- **Decide** with a shared, auditable record — not another slide deck that dies in Slack
- **Correct** reality as it changes — without losing history or inventing facts

Every piece of content must reinforce this positioning and stay **honest about what the POC proves today** versus what is on the arc.

---

## POC positioning (non-negotiable)

### What we are

- A **living belief-graph** of the organization — not a truth oracle
- An **organizational compiler** — conversation and (soon) documents → structured, governed understanding
- A **shared operating picture** — Structure (what exists) and Dynamics (what’s moving), one graph, two lenses
- **Governance-native** — decisions leave a receipt: who, why, what-for
- **Honest by construction** — Stated / Inferred / Unknown; confidence and sources visible where the product supports it

### What we are not (never imply in POC content)

- “AI that knows the truth about your company”
- Your system of record for emails, PDFs, or Slack
- Production-ready for paying customers (deploy is **held** for security audit)
- Fully shipped: file/comms drop, polished “ask the org” Q&A, full team propose→approve dogfood — **call these “on the POC arc” unless STATUS says live**

### One-line POC promise (use internally; adapt for external copy)

> **Bizdaptive holds one honest, living, shared, evidence-backed picture of your organization — and it gets truer the more you use it.**

## Brand voice

### We are

- Analytical and **business-first**
- Practical — operations, revenue, delivery, people, not “AI magic”
- Strategic — decisions, accountability, consequences
- Executive-friendly — short summaries, clear stakes
- Confident but **honest** — we say what works and what’s next
- Insightful — we explain *why* decisions fail, not just that data is messy

### We are not

- Salesy or urgency-driven (“Buy now”, “Limited offer”)
- Buzzword-heavy (“synergy”, “revolutionary AI”, “digital transformation”)
- Generic AI thought leadership
- Technical for its own sake (no LAG/α2/ChangeSet in customer-facing copy)
- Overclaiming POC scope

---

## Writing principles

Every article must:

✔ Teach one business idea  
✔ Solve **one** business problem  
✔ Explain **why** it happens  
✔ Explain **impact** (time, money, risk, morale)  
✔ Explain **what better looks like**  
✔ Connect to **decisions** — who decides, on what evidence, with what record  

Never lead with features. Lead with **Monday morning**.

### Language swap

| Avoid | Prefer |
|--------|--------|
| “Our platform provides…” | “Operations leads often spend Monday reconciling three versions of the same number before anyone can decide.” |
| “AI-powered insights” | “A recommendation tied to who said what, and how sure we are” |
| “Single source of truth” (unqualified) | “One governed picture the team can challenge and correct” |
| “The system knows…” | “The system reflects what was stated, inferred, or still unknown” |
| Dashboard-first | Graph-first, decision-first, receipt-first |

## Audience

**Primary:** Founder, CEO, COO, Head of Operations, Head of Sales, Regional Manager, Finance lead, Product lead  

**Secondary:** Business analysts, team leads, implementation-minded operators  

**Reader outcome:**  
> “This company understands how decisions actually get made — and where they break.”  

**Not:**  
> “This company has AI.”

# BLOG WRITING SKILL (POC-aligned)

## Objective

Educate. Build trust. Show we understand **operational and strategic decisions** — not sell software.

Readers arrive with a **business problem**, not a shopping list.

---

## Blog formula

### 1. Powerful title

Not: *What Is Operational Visibility*  
Instead: *Your On-Time Number Is Fine in the Sheet. Your Team Still Doesn’t Know Who Owns the Fix.*

### 2. Subtitle — one sentence: the single problem this article solves.

### 3. Executive summary — 3–5 sentences. A CEO should get the point in under 30 seconds.

### 4. Hook — realistic scenario; no “In today’s fast-paced business environment.”

### 5. Business problem — symptoms, pain. **Do not mention Bizdaptive here.**

### 6. Root cause analysis

### 7. Business impact

### 8. Better approach — modern practice, still not selling

### 9. Bizdaptive perspective — POC-honest

### 10. Actionable takeaways — checklist usable without buying

### 11. CTA (soft) — never Buy now / Start free / Limited offer

---

## Blog rules

| Rule | Standard |
|------|----------|
| Length | 1,500–2,500 words |
| Reading level | Simple business English |
| Paragraphs | Max ~4 lines |
| Headings | Every 200–300 words |

# USE CASE WRITING SKILL (POC-aligned)

## Use case structure

- Title — Industry + decision problem
- Business overview
- Current situation
- Business challenges
- Existing process
- Problems with existing process
- Desired future state
- Bizdaptive solution (POC-honest) — Capability → Decision → Value
- Workflow
- Surfaces used
- Decision examples
- KPIs (before/after, believable)
- Business outcomes
- Applicable industries
- Next steps (POC / next / later)

---

## POC vocabulary (quick reference)

| Use | Avoid (in customer copy) |
|-----|---------------------------|
| Company Graph, Structure, Dynamics | “Unified dashboard” |
| First-decision receipt | “AI-generated summary” |
| Add-context / correction | “Auto-sync from Slack” (unless live) |
| Governed change, who/why/what-for | “Blockchain” / buzzwords |
| Belief, confidence, evidence | “The AI is correct” |
| On the POC arc / coming | Silent implication something is shipped |
